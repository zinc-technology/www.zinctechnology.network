It Doesn't Have To Suck

Developing Ethically Owned and Operated Restorative Economic Organizations

The Zinc Technology Network (ZTN) is composed of restorative socioeconomic organizations. We develops these organizations through free access to whatever information, support, and low-cost capital we can make safely available.

The Philosophy

Just Power Is Derived From The Consent Of The Governed

Restorative organizations bind investors, customers, and workers together in a mutually beneficial manner. They ensure each person within the system has representation that establishes the norms, agreements, and rules the organization operates under. And they make it safe to join, stay or go without fear or loss.

Put On Our Mask

We care for ourselves, because if we do not care for ourselves; we cannot care for others. We care for others so that they may, in time, care for themselves. Because when we support people so that they can care for themselves, they care for others in turn.

Remove The Knife

We stand on the shoulders of giants, and also on the backs and necks of those whose consent was not sought in our current socioeconomic context. To rectify this, we prioritize the needs of those who endured and persisted despite multi-generational socioeconomic exploitation.

Take Small, Safe Steps; Together

Restorative organizations seek to go far, not fast. We understand that "to go fast, go alone; to go far, go together." To go together we slow down to match the pace of those who we would leave behind. We take pauses to breathe, step back, and follow others lead.

The History

ZTN was founded in 2015 by Zee Spencer. He'd ran his own microconsultancy back in the midwest, and wanted to try it out in the the San Francisco Bay Area Market. In six months, ZTN grew to 5 part-time contractors across 3 clients. What could go wrong? Zee was in over his head. Consultancies don't scale linearly. Things fell apart. He helped his team find new gigs and let everyone go.

He spent the next two years working in FinTech, burning out hardÂ so Jamie Dimon can afford a slightly larger yach. In 2017, a bit tired of the madhouse that is venture backed, 10x growth-mandated companies Zee rebooted ZTN. But this time, he had help. He brought on Jennifer Tu and Betsy Haibel, with the goal of operating as a worker-cooperative. Together, they launched Cohere. To this day, Cohere provides Zee and Jennifer with a sustainable living, while Betsy provides senior engineering leadership at the Long Term Stock Exchange.

Zee began researching other mechanisms for creating a healthy, sustainable, restorative way to work; and found two communities that showed better way: the Sustainable Economies Law Center and Zebras Unite.

Enamored with the East Bay Permanent Real Estate Cooperative, Zee found a use for ZTN again. He took 15% of his assets and said "Fuck it, let's light this shit on fire." He brought on Vivek Narayan, a former client of ZTN. Together, they acquired a small portfolio of software products that would never achieve results that would make their investors happy. With real products used by real customers, they lured together a team of professional designers, support team members, and engineers to steward them. They formed a worker-self directed technology product and service company: ZINC. (Note: ZINC does not stand for "Zee Is eNtranced by Coops.")

But there is still more to do. The knife must be removed. None are free until all are free.

In 2020, ZTN began lending small cash amounts at low interest rates to members of under-capitalized communities so they can self-determine their own best way forward. Starting a non-profit? Go for it! Taking the coop route? Great! Think you can dance with the angels and swim with the sharks? We ain't gonna stop ya.

We'll see how it goes.
